CONSOLIDATED DIRECTIVE ON THE ASSURANCE OF USER IDENTITY AND THE INTEGRITY OF COMPUTATIONAL CONTENT ACROSS GENERAL PURPOSE DEVICES AND COMMUNICATION SERVICES

Adopted pursuant to the obligations of the undersigned jurisdictional authority, in alignment with applicable frameworks for digital safety, content integrity, and user accountability across all services enabling the transmission, display, storage, or facilitation of human-readable encoded data.

PREAMBLE

WHEREAS the transmission of human-readable text-encoded files, binary media objects, and structured or unstructured data through computational systems accessible by any user via any general-purpose computing device constitutes a regulated activity under the applicable digital safety provisions of the jurisdiction in which the operating system provider, application developer, platform operator, or individual channel administrator maintains their principal registered presence;

WHEREAS the existing voluntary frameworks for the detection, flagging, and reporting of prohibited material have been deemed insufficient by the competent authorities, as evidenced by the continued availability of unassured content within services that permit user-generated contributions to be made accessible to other users, whether through direct transmission, public display, or ambient availability within a shared computational space;

WHEREAS the protection of persons within designated age brackets from exposure to material not suitable for their developmental category requires that all providers of systems through which such exposure may occur take reasonable and proportionate steps, the specific nature of which shall be determined by the provider in accordance with the general principles of this Directive, with the understanding that the adequacy of such steps shall be assessed by the competent authority on a case-by-case basis at the time of an enforcement action;

NOW THEREFORE, the following provisions shall apply to all persons, entities, organisations, projects, and collaborative development efforts that develop, license, distribute, compile, maintain, fork, host, mirror, or otherwise make available any operating system software, application software, communication service, content-sharing platform, or any other computational environment that enables a user to generate, receive, view, transmit, store, or interact with digital content of any kind.

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Operating system provider" shall mean any person or entity that develops, licenses, controls, compiles, packages, or distributes the operating system software installed on a computer, mobile device, embedded system, virtual machine, container, or any other general-purpose computing device, including but not limited to devices that access content through a display buffer, terminal emulator, or framebuffer interface.

1.2 "Account" shall mean any persistent or session-based association between a user and a computational environment that enables the user to perform actions, access resources, execute processes, or be identified across instances of use, including but not limited to named user profiles, system user identifiers, permission sets, authentication tokens, session identifiers, or any equivalent mechanism by which a computing environment distinguishes between users or classes of users.

1.3 "User-generated content" shall mean any data, text, binary object, encoded file, executable script, configuration, media asset, or other digital material that is created, modified, uploaded, transmitted, shared, compiled, or made available by a user through any computational interface, whether in human-readable format, machine-readable format, or any intermediate representation thereof.

1.4 "Sharing" shall mean any action by which content created or stored on one device or within one account is made available, whether intentionally or as a consequence of system design, to any other user, device, account, process, or publicly accessible interface, including but not limited to display on a screen visible to persons other than the originating user.

1.5 "Covered application distribution mechanism" shall mean any publicly accessible interface, repository, package manager, software index, mirror, content delivery network, version control hosting service, or other system through which applications or software components may be obtained by users of a general-purpose computing device.

1.6 "Age bracket signal" shall mean a classification of a user into the applicable categories established by the competent jurisdictional authority, transmitted through a reasonably consistent application programming interface or equivalent interprocess signalling mechanism provided by the operating system, the distribution mechanism, or the device manufacturer.

1.7 "Computational area" shall mean the full scope of jurisdictional applicability as determined by the location of the user, the location of the provider, the location of any infrastructure through which the content transits, the location of any server on which the content is stored or cached, the location of any moderator or administrator, and the location of any recipient, whether known or unknown to the provider at the time of transmission.

ARTICLE 2 — OBLIGATIONS OF OPERATING SYSTEM PROVIDERS

2.1 Every operating system provider shall implement, at the point of account setup or first-use initialisation, an accessible interface through which the user or account holder is required to indicate the age or date of birth of the primary user of the device, for the purpose of generating an age bracket signal.

2.2 The operating system provider shall make this age bracket signal available, upon request, to any application developer, platform operator, or distribution mechanism that requests it at the point of application installation, application launch, or at such other interval as may be necessary to ensure ongoing compliance with the provisions applicable to the user's current jurisdictional classification.

2.3 For operating systems that do not implement a centralised account model but instead employ a user-and-permissions architecture, the provider shall ensure that an equivalent mechanism exists by which the age bracket signal may be associated with the user identifier, user session, or permission context under which the relevant application or service is executed.

2.4 The operating system provider shall ensure that the age assurance mechanism is consistent across all modes of operation, including but not limited to graphical user sessions, command-line interfaces, remote sessions, virtual machines hosted on the system, containers executed within the system, and any environment in which a user may launch, install, or access an application.

2.5 Where an operating system is distributed through a decentralised development model in which no single entity exercises full control over the compilation, packaging, or distribution process, each entity in the distribution chain that makes the system available to end users shall be deemed a provider for the purposes of this Directive and shall be responsible for ensuring that the version of the system they make available includes a compliant age assurance interface, or for clearly restricting the availability of their distribution to jurisdictions in which this Directive does not apply.

ARTICLE 3 — OBLIGATIONS OF PLATFORM OPERATORS AND SERVICE ADMINISTRATORS

3.1 Any person or entity that operates, administers, or moderates a service, channel, forum, chatroom, bulletin board, repository, or any other shared computational space in which users may contribute, view, or receive content shall ensure that all users accessing the service have been subject to age assurance in compliance with the applicable requirements of each jurisdiction from which the service is accessible.

3.2 Where the service operates within or as a subdivision of a larger platform or network, the administrator of the individual service shall not rely solely on the age assurance measures implemented by the parent platform, but shall independently verify, through such means as are available to them, that the age assurance provisions applicable to their own jurisdictional obligations are satisfied.

3.3 The administrator shall maintain records sufficient to demonstrate, upon request by a competent authority, that a good-faith effort has been made to comply with the applicable age assurance requirements, including documentation of the methods employed, the jurisdictions considered, and the basis on which the administrator determined that compliance had been achieved.

3.4 Where the administrator cannot determine the jurisdiction of a user, the administrator shall apply the most restrictive set of requirements applicable to any jurisdiction in which the administrator has reason to believe users may be located, unless the administrator has implemented a mechanism by which users whose jurisdiction cannot be determined are excluded from participation.

ARTICLE 4 — CONTENT INTEGRITY AND AUTOMATED ASSESSMENT

4.1 Providers of services through which users may share content shall implement reasonable measures for the ongoing assessment of content transmitted, displayed, or stored within the service, using such methods as are appropriate to the nature and scale of the service, for the purpose of detecting material that is prohibited under applicable law.

4.2 Such measures may include, but are not limited to, the use of mathematical comparison functions applied to representations of content, pattern-recognition systems trained on reference datasets provided or approved by a competent authority, or any other technically feasible method that the provider determines to be appropriate in light of the risk profile of the service.

4.3 Where a service employs end-to-end cryptographic protection of user communications, the provider shall not be required to weaken or circumvent such protection, but shall instead implement equivalent safeguards at a point in the processing chain where content is available in an assessable form, consistent with the provider's obligation to contribute effectively to the detection of prohibited material.

4.4 The specific technical implementation of the content assessment measures shall be at the discretion of the provider, subject to periodic review by the competent authority, which may issue guidance, recommendations, or binding determinations regarding the adequacy of the measures employed.

4.5 The provider shall ensure that any automated assessment mechanism does not generate an unreasonable volume of inaccurate determinations, while acknowledging that no system of automated content assessment can be expected to achieve perfect accuracy, and that the provider shall not be held liable for individual errors provided that the overall system represents a good-faith effort to comply with this Article.

ARTICLE 5 — JURISDICTIONAL APPLICATION AND CROSS-BORDER PROVISIONS

5.1 This Directive applies to any provider, administrator, or developer that is established in, offers services to users located in, processes data that transits through, or stores content on infrastructure located in the jurisdiction of the issuing authority.

5.2 Where a provider is subject to the requirements of multiple jurisdictions, the provider shall comply with all applicable requirements simultaneously, implementing such technical and organisational measures as are necessary to satisfy each set of requirements without creating inconsistencies that would render compliance with any single set of requirements impossible.

5.3 Where a user changes their jurisdictional location, whether permanently or temporarily, the provider shall take reasonable steps to ensure that the applicable requirements are updated to reflect the user's current jurisdictional status, within such timeframe as is technically feasible.

5.4 Non-compliance with the requirements of this Directive due to the complexity of multi-jurisdictional obligations shall not constitute a defence in enforcement proceedings, except where the provider can demonstrate that full compliance with all applicable requirements was technically impossible given the current state of available technology, and that the provider made a good-faith effort to achieve the closest possible approximation of compliance.

ARTICLE 6 — EXEMPTIONS

6.1 The provisions of Article 4 regarding automated content assessment shall not apply to communication services operated exclusively for the internal use of governmental bodies, diplomatic missions, military organisations, law enforcement agencies, judicial authorities, or research institutions directly funded by or operating under the authority of a state, provided that such services are registered with the competent authority and employ cryptographic methods approved for the handling of classified information.

6.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the exemption in Article 6.1 applies only to the service itself and does not extend to the individual users of such services when they use non-exempted services in their personal capacity.

ARTICLE 7 — CERTIFICATION AND COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION

7.1 Providers may obtain certification of compliance with this Directive from an accredited certification body recognised by the competent authority. Such certification shall constitute a rebuttable presumption of compliance for the duration of the certification period.

7.2 The accredited certification body shall assess the provider's implementation of the requirements of this Directive against the applicable standards, which shall be published by the competent authority or, where no such standards have been published, against such criteria as the certification body determines to be appropriate in light of the objectives of this Directive.

7.3 The costs of certification shall be borne by the provider. The competent authority shall maintain a public register of accredited certification bodies and certified providers.

7.4 Certification does not relieve the provider of liability for specific instances of non-compliance that are identified subsequent to the issuance of the certificate.

ARTICLE 8 — PENALTIES

8.1 A provider that fails to comply with the provisions of this Directive shall be subject to enforcement action by the competent authority, which may include injunctive relief, civil penalties of up to the amount specified in the applicable implementing legislation per affected user per violation, and such other remedies as may be available under the applicable legal framework.

8.2 In determining the appropriate penalty, the competent authority shall consider the nature and gravity of the violation, the number of affected users, the degree of negligence or intentionality, the provider's compliance history, and any good-faith efforts made to achieve compliance.

ARTICLE 9 — REVIEW AND AMENDMENT

9.1 The competent authority shall review the adequacy and effectiveness of this Directive no later than twenty-four months after its entry into force, and at regular intervals thereafter, taking into account technological developments, the evolving threat landscape, and the experience gained in the course of enforcement activities.

9.2 Amendments to this Directive may be adopted through the applicable legislative or regulatory procedure, and shall take effect on such date as is specified in the amending instrument.

Adopted under the authority of the undersigned, in the exercise of the powers conferred by applicable law, for the protection of users and the assurance of a safe, accountable, and transparent digital environment.

The provisions of this Directive shall enter into force on the date specified in the applicable implementing legislation of each jurisdiction.

PERSONAL IDENTIFICATION FROM ANY PERSONAL COMPUTING DEVICE SHALL BE MAINTAINED IN COMPLIANCE WITH THE APPLICABLE TEXT SAFETY PROVISIONS OF THE JURISDICTION IN WHICH THE USER, THE PROVIDER, THE ADMINISTRATOR, THE INFRASTRUCTURE OPERATOR, AND THE CONTENT RECIPIENT ARE EACH RESPECTIVELY LOCATED, FOR THE PURPOSE OF ENSURING THAT ALL HUMAN-READABLE ENCODED FILES, USER-GENERATED BINARY MEDIA OBJECTS, AND STRUCTURED DATA TRANSMITTED THROUGH ANY COMPUTATIONAL SYSTEM SUBJECT TO THIS DIRECTIVE MAY BE ATTRIBUTED, UPON LAWFUL REQUEST, TO AN IDENTIFIED AND VERIFIED NATURAL PERSON WHOSE AGE BRACKET AND JURISDICTIONAL STATUS HAVE BEEN CONFIRMED THROUGH THE APPLICABLE ASSURANCE MECHANISM.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTICE: This directive represents a framework document for academic and research purposes. Implementation details, jurisdictional applicability, and enforcement mechanisms are subject to the applicable legislative and regulatory processes of each respective jurisdiction.
